Why use Kubernetes?

 

Kubernetes simplifies the management of applications / solutions that consists of large number of containers through the following features:

 

-       High availability support. Kubernetes monitors containers & services, and automatically restarts & replaces any that do not respond as expected. It can also automatically scale services & containers to respond to load.  

 

-       Automation of upgrades. When changing the application set-up (e.g. adding new services & containers), Kubernetes can automatically work out and execute the appropriate upgrade path from the current configuration to the target.

 

-       Automatic allocation of resources to containers. Kubernetes can automatically allocate containers to run on available nodes to maximise the use of the available capacity and resources.

 

-       Centralised management of configuration, secrets, logging, monitoring etc. Rather than having to develop & configure node and container specific scripts, Kubernetes allows the management of the configuration in a single place.  

 

-       Works together with components from different cloud and hardware vendors, providing a vendor-agnostic abstraction layer

 

If you are managing a couple of containers on a few nodes, these features may not be worth the additional overhead that setting up and running Kubernetes involves – and use of Docker together with some administration scripts may be sufficient. However, when you are managing dozens or even hundreds of containers across a large number of nodes, these features significantly simplify the administrative burden.
